Examples of Design Princlples

White space and Hicks law

Unity

You might be wondering how Unity applies Hick's law, along with white space, when you first go to the site you will be greeted with a mostly white spaced site that then also displays its most prominant products, Unity Pro, Unity Reflects, Unity Build Server and more.

These options are close within proximity of each other, but most importantly, they display their best products up front and don't give you to many options to choose from. The white space use is also interesting because for learning such as automotive or film, they offer you learn more but don't take away from the product options below them.

Visual Hierachy and Fitt's law

Pluralsight

Pluralsight is quite unique in the way that it has some content that is related, but at the same time has the call to action buttons more prominant up front. This means that you will notice right away that the first thing they want you to see is the banner, build better (skills, processes, teams, products, etc).

Then call to action which to view plans or try for free, they want you to see the plans but don't make it impossible to use both. thye use vivid colors from the background they use as a banner as well and then below without scrolling, you can see trending technologies that you might want to look into.

Fitt's law and contrast

Spotify

In this example, I chose Spotify, not because it was in the example of universal web design princles, but because it has changed since it was talked about in 2019, and how it more closely resembles the PARC rules as well as Fitt's law.

Another huge aspect to the Spotify site is the way it handles contrast compared to the other sites. They decided to use vibrant colors that contrast say an album cover. Allowing for you to better visually see the site you're on.
